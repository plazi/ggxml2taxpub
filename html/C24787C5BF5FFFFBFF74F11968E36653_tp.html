Paloniella feana ( Distant, 1904 )

Yeshwanth, H. M., Chérot, F. & Henry, T. J. Paloniella feana Distant 1904

( Figs. 2F , 6H , 10B , 14 A–D)

Isometopus feanus Distant, 1904: 484 (as new species, DV); Schuh 1995: 9 (catalogue); Schuh 2002 –2013 (online catalogue). Paloniella feana : Akingbohungbe 1996: 102 , 103 (new combination).

Diagnosis . Small, length 2 mm , shiny, yellowish brown to brown, with shiny elongate setae on the dorsum. Head and lateral areas of pronotum and corium paler; membrane pale fuscous, with the lateral and apical areas greyish; eyes pale brown to dark brown; head with a distinct rounded transverse impression between eyes. Antennal segment I reddish brown or dark brown; segment II pale brown, apex darker; segment III and IV dark brown. Posterior margins of eyes slightly projecting backward to touch the anterior margin of the pronotum; ocelli closer to eyes than to each other. Pronotum finely punctate, with short longitudinal impression on anterior area; scutellum triangularly foveate at base, finely transversely rugose beyond; corium finely rugosely punctate. Male genitalia : as in Figs. 14 A–D. Detailed measurements in Table 1 .
