Diagnosis. Very similar to L. bipunctata but different as follows: Slightly larger, wing length 3.0‒ 4.3mm . Male: Frontal vitta deep black in contrast to wholly silvery white fronto-orbital and parafacial plates. Mesonotum mainly shiny black through thin cover of brown pruinosity, but a contrasting pattern of dense silvery white pruinosity (with a bluish tinge) covers the postpronotal lobes and notopleura and extends further to the anterior wing base along with a pair of marks in front of the scutellum. Vein R 4+5 on basal half between radial node and crossvein r‒m with 1‒3 dorsal setulae. Sternite V and hypopygium as in Figs 4A‒C . Female: Separated from female L. bipunctata by the same differences in colour as given for the male. See Table 1 for ovipositor characters which coincide with those of L. bipunctata .

Distribution. Endemic Canarian species recorded from Gran Canaria, Tenerife, La Gomera and La Palma.
